## Anomaly Detection Rules

### Rule 1 – Approval Rate Too Low

Threshold:
Approval Rate < 70%

Risk:

* Analysts may be overly strict
* Potential loss of good applicants

Alert Severity:
WARNING

Recommended Action:

* Review rejected applications
* Audit analyst decisions
* Verify rule configuration

---

### Rule 2 – Approval Rate Too High

Threshold:
Approval Rate > 85%

Risk:

* Analysts may be overly lenient
* Increased portfolio risk

Alert Severity:
WARNING

Recommended Action:

* Review approved applications
* Audit approval rationale
* Verify risk controls

---

### Rule 3 – Sudden Daily Change

Threshold:

Approval Rate Change > 5% in 24 Hours

Alert Severity:
MEDIUM

Investigation:

* Review analyst activity
* Review system changes
* Review application mix

Status:
ACTIVE

### Analyst Comparison Logic

Analyst Approval Rate Difference =
Analyst Rate – Team Average

Alert Trigger:

Difference > 10%

Examples:

Analyst A:

Difference = +2.59%

Analyst B:
Approval Rate = 92%
Difference = +12.59%
Status = ALERT

Analyst C:

Difference = -14.41%
Status = ALERT

Recommended Actions:

* Review decision samples

* Conduct refresher training if required

## Decision Quality Correlation

Objective:

Determine whether analyst strictness correlates with application quality.

Metrics:

### Strict Analysts

Definition:

Approval Rate < 70%

Track:

* Approved applications
* Default rate
* Delinquency rate

### Lenient Analysts

Definition:

Approval Rate > 85%

Track:

* Approved applications
* Default rate
* Delinquency rate

Analysis Outcome:

If strict analysts have significantly lower default rates:
Review approval standards.

If lenient analysts have significantly higher default rates:
Review approval controls.

If no meaningful difference exists:
Maintain current thresholds.

Status:
MONITORED
