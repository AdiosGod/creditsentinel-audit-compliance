# Red Flag User Guide

## What is a Red Flag?

A Red Flag is an automated warning generated when an applicant meets predefined financial risk conditions.

Red Flags assist analysts by highlighting applications that require additional review.

---

# Red Flag 1 – High Debt-to-Income Ratio

Meaning

The applicant spends more than 45% of income on debt repayments.

Why is it risky?

High debt reduces repayment capacity.

Recommended Action

Request supporting income documents and perform manual review.

---

# Red Flag 2 – Multiple Credit Inquiries

Meaning

The applicant has submitted multiple credit applications recently.

Why is it risky?

May indicate urgent borrowing or financial distress.

Recommended Action

Request explanation from the applicant.

---

# Red Flag 3 – Payment History Issues

Meaning

The applicant has significant late payment history.

Why is it risky?

Past repayment behaviour predicts future repayment behaviour.

Recommended Action

Review repayment history before approval.

---

# Red Flag 4 – Low Credit Score

Meaning

Applicant's credit score is below 600.

Why is it risky?

Poor credit history increases lending risk.

Recommended Action

Escalate to Senior Analyst.

---

# Response Options

Analysts may:

- Request Additional Documentation
- Approve with Conditions
- Escalate for Senior Review
- Reject Application

---

# Example

Scenario:

Applicant has:

- Credit Score: 575
- DTI: 52%

Generated Flags:

✓ High Debt-to-Income
✓ Low Credit Score

Recommended Action:

Escalate to Senior Analyst before final decision.
